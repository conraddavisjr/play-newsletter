TOTAL SOLAR ECLIPSE
Look up! On August 21, 2017, it will be the first time in 100 years that a total solar eclipse crosses the whole US.
 
To celebrate this once in a lifetime event, Google and the University of California at Berkeley are partnering for the Eclipse Megamovie Project that encourages citizen scientists around the country to help gather images of the sun and its atmosphere, the corona. Volunteers armed with standard photography equipment—a camera, telephoto lens, and tripod—will capture photos of the eclipse as the moon’s shadow passes over their part of the country. Once this huge dataset is collected, it’s Google’s turn. We’ll use our technology to algorithmically align and process the submitted images to create a continuous view of the eclipse: the Eclipse Megamovie.
 
To learn more about the upcoming solar eclipse, we recommend that you check out this book ("Your Guide to the 2017 Total Solar Eclipse") from Astronomy Magazine editor Michael Bakich. You can also read more ("American Eclipse: A Nation's Epic Race to Catch the Shadow of the Moon and Win the Glory of the World") about how a solar eclipse in 1878 dominated late nineteenth-century American astronomy.

WHAT'S TRENDING

Winter came in July as "Game of Thrones" season 6 reigned over the Top TV Shows in the Google Play store, along with HBO Now as the top trending app this month, as fans got battle-ready for season 7. Google Play readers also went for intrigue as Daniel Silva’s newest book, "House of Spies: A Novel," took the top spot on the new book chart.
 
A lot of music fans experienced triple digits in July with Jay-Z’s newest album "4:44" coming in as the top purchased album for the month and things got heated with Luis Fonsi & Daddy Yankee‘s "Despacito (Remix) (feat. Justin Bieber)" as the most streamed song.
 
 
 

TOP TRENDING APPS IN July
 
 
HBO Now
 
The much anticipated premiere of “Game of Thrones” season 7 on July 16th caused a spike in user interest in HBO Now.
 
Flipagram
 
Leading up to Father’s Day in mid-June, the app was updated with seasonal filters and themes so that users could easily create fun family videos for dads on their special day.
 
Starz
 
Users downloaded the app leading up to the season premiere of “Power” at the end of June. With a first month free subscription option, Starz makes it easy to stream shows online.
 
HOOKED
 
Android users are getting “hooked” on this storytelling app that are in the format of a chat conversation. This fresh take on reading makes it hard to put down your phone.
 
EyeEm
 
This online marketplace for photographers to share and sell their work released a major update in June that allowed photographers to more easily sell photos on the EyeEm Market.
