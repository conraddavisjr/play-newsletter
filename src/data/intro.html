Empowering more teens to code

We recently teamed up with Girls Who Code this week to help young women develop and maintain interest in Computer Science by promoting a new series of books on Google Play. Published by Penguin, the new collection on Google Play will include 13 books focused on coding, such as nonfiction book, Girls Who Code: Learn to Code and Change the World, and a fiction book, The Friendship Code. You can also expect to see a coding-themed journal, activity book, board books, and more! Separately, you can also find some recommended books for coding here.

Back to school

Saying goodbye to summer and hello to a new school year can be rough, but we’ve got the best tools to ease the transition. See our new editorial page of clever apps that we recommend, such as NeuroNation - Brain Training, Mindly, Headspace: Guided Meditation & Mindfulness and more, to help you dive into your studies feeling rested, centered, and focused.

Students can also rent or purchase textbooks through Google Play. Right now we have sales on popular textbooks from McGraw Hill and Elsevier, as well as deals on titles in dozens of other subjects. Reading your textbooks on Google Play has tons of other benefits -- you can use sticky notes and highlights within the text to call out key points, sync your reading across devices, and you can even push your notes to Google Drive and use Docs to create a study guide.

WHAT’S TRENDING

Music fans can’t get enough of Jay-Z’s newest album “4:44” and the bilingual song of the summer “Despacito (Remix) (feat.
